The long decline of manufacturing in Southwest Virginia

How two decades of deindustrialization reshaped five Appalachian counties

Southwest Virginia's manufacturing sector entered the 21st century already under strain. Between 2000 and 2022, the five counties of Wise, Lee, Scott, Dickenson, and Buchanan collectively lost nearly half their manufacturing jobs — a slow hemorrhage punctuated by the sharp shock of the Great Recession. By 2022, fewer than 22,000 workers remained in manufacturing, down from over 42,000 at the start of the millennium.

The pattern is consistent across counties: a gradual decline through the early 2000s, a steep drop between 2008 and 2010, a partial and short-lived recovery, then a resumed slide through the 2010s. No county was spared. The losses reflect both global forces — import competition, automation — and local vulnerability: a regional economy with few alternative industries to absorb displaced workers.
